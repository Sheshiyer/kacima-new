KĀCIMA — PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENT (PRD)

1. Product Overview

Kācima is an immersive sanctuary on Kanakapura Road in Bengaluru, created to help people step out of the speed of city life and return to stillness, breath, and elemental experience.
The website is not a typical informational site. It is a narrative-led, motion-driven digital sanctuary that mirrors the feeling of arriving at Kācima: slow, ceremonial, sensory, grounding.

The website functions as a long-form immersive scroll composed of seven narrative “chapters.” Each chapter builds emotional depth, visual identity, and conceptual grounding.
The site is purely informational in Phase 1 — no booking engine, no user accounts, no dashboards.

The digital experience must feel alive, handcrafted, and spiritually resonant without adopting any religious overtones. The tone is contemplative, poetic, slow, and sensory.

⸻

2. Objectives & Success Criteria

Primary Objectives
•	Communicate the emotional identity and story of Kācima.
•	Evoke a sense of sanctuary, stillness, and elemental connection.
•	Introduce visitors to the core offerings and spatial philosophy.
•	Provide a contact channel for sessions or collaborations.
•	Build a strong digital foundation for future expansions.

Success Criteria
•	Visitors feel emotionally shifted (calmer, grounded) during and after scrolling.
•	The site stands out as an unconventional, memorable experience.
•	Narrative depth is maintained and not diluted by typical UI patterns.
•	Performance is smooth despite motion-heavy interactions.
•	Visitors recall the space as sacred, nature-aligned, and sensorial.

⸻

3. Target Audience
•	Urban individuals seeking spiritual, emotional, or physical rejuvenation.
•	People interested in Yoga, meditation, sound healing, energy work.
•	Creatives, healers, artists exploring residencies or collaborations.
•	Individuals wanting a break from the city’s intensity.
•	Visitors seeking experiences defined by intention and breath.

The audience values:
•	sensory environments
•	authenticity
•	nature-led experiences
•	spaciousness
•	narrative richness

⸻

4. Content Structure (7 Narrative Chapters)

Each section must function like a “scene” in a ritual journey.
1.	The Threshold — Emotional introduction and brand presence.
2.	The Tree & The Name — Origin mythology and symbolic identity.
3.	The Land & Sanctuary — Physical transition from city → grove.
4.	Practices & Offerings — Sensory descriptions of experiences.
5.	A Day at Kācima — A step-by-step visual journey.
6.	Community & Future Gatherings — Collected energy of collaborators.
7.	Visit & Connect — Emotional closing and contact channel.

⸻

5. Functional Requirements

Core Features
•	Smooth long-form scroll experience
•	Section-based animations triggered by scroll
•	Subtle parallax and atmospheric transitions
•	Contact form with text input (name/email/message)
•	Accessibility support for reduced motion
•	Mobile-responsive narrative design

Non-Functional Requirements
•	Must load quickly despite textures and animations
•	Animations should not induce dizziness or fast motion events
•	All transitions must feel hand-crafted, organic

Non-Features (Explicit Exclusions for Phase 1)
•	No booking engine
•	No payment system
•	No user accounts
•	No social media feed
•	No blog
•	No CMS necessary in Phase 1
•	No audio autoplay

⸻

6. Motion Principles (High-Level Requirements)

Each motion must follow these guidelines:
•	Slow, meditative pacing
•	Organic shapes revealing through masks
•	Hand-drawn imperfections preferred
•	Motion always supports narrative
•	Never distracts, never loops aggressively
•	Inspired by breath, water ripples, leaf movement, unfolding branches

Specific motion per section will be detailed in design.md.

⸻

7. Platform Requirements
•	Web (desktop + mobile)
•	Smooth performance in Chrome, Safari, Firefox
•	Progressive enhancement for older devices
•	Static or semi-static site architecture preferred for reliability

⸻

8. Technical Considerations
•	Use compressed textures with careful scaling
•	Use Lottie for nuanced line animation when appropriate
•	Use SVG for ornamental linework
•	Avoid large video backgrounds; use short subtle loops if needed
•	Prefer CSS transforms for performance-oriented motion

⸻

9. Future Scalability

The site may later expand to include:
•	Retreat booking system
•	Event calendar
•	Blog / community hub
•	Shop
•	Member portal

All current architecture should allow layering these without redesigning the entire narrative structure.

⸻

10. Constraints
•	Must adhere to brand palette and aesthetic
•	Motion cannot be overwhelming
•	No corporate or run-of-mill UI blocks
•	Must preserve sensory and emotional tone
