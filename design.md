KĀCIMA — DESIGN SPECIFICATION DOCUMENT (DESIGN.md)

1. Design Philosophy

The Kācima digital experience is a visual sanctuary.
The design must feel:
•	handcrafted
•	ritualistic
•	earthy
•	slow
•	intentional
•	textural
•	ancient-yet-modern

The core blend is:
•	Art Deco geometry (structure, symmetry, frames)
•	Art Nouveau organic curves (flow, leaves, roots, ornamentation)
•	Grunge textures (earth, clay, subtle imperfections)

No part of the website should resemble a template.
Every block should feel like a chapter in a visual poem.

⸻

2. Color Palette

Primary:
•	Olive Earth (#5A5A32 approximate)
•	Clay Peach (#D4B08C approximate)

Secondary:
•	Soft Cream
•	Dark Soil Brown

All textures, borders, illustrations, and typography must remain within these tonal families.

⸻

3. Typography

Headlines
•	Serif type inspired by Art Deco geometry
•	Wide caps, soft curves, ceremonial pacing
•	Visual weight: Medium → Bold

Body Text
•	Warm humanist sans serif
•	Generous line spacing and soft character rhythm
•	Appears gentle, quiet, readable

Microcopy
•	Use clay-peach for emphasis
•	Minimal punctuation

⸻

4. Texture System

Textures define the “soul” of the site:
•	Matte grain overlays
•	Clay-surface textures
•	Pressed-paper noise
•	Organic blemishes
•	Faint leaf or bark patterns

Textures should never overpower text; they are background “breath.”

⸻

5. Iconography

Icons must follow hand-drawn aesthetics:
•	Line thickness consistent with Deco frames
•	Leaf motifs for natural elements
•	Root-like curves for transitions
•	Minimal, not illustrative

⸻

6. Layout System

General Layout Rules
•	Asymmetric where appropriate
•	No traditional cards or grids
•	Sections unfold like scenes
•	Avoid rectangular white boxes
•	Use organic edges or ornamental frames

Spacing
•	Generous vertical breathing space
•	No tight clusters
•	Text must feel like it’s resting lightly on the page

Section Dividers
•	Deco gate lines
•	Nouveau botanical curls
•	Grunge-textured fades

⸻

7. Motion Design (Detailed Per Section)

Section 1 — The Threshold (Hero)
•	Logo appears as a solid emblem
•	Subtle inhale-exhale pulse (breathing)
•	Background reveals gradually through organic masks
•	Slow parallax of shadowed nature textures
•	Motion cue: “the sanctuary begins gently”

⸻

Section 2 — The Tree & The Name
•	Vertical unfolding — like branches growing downward and upward
•	Roots appear through line animation
•	Soft shimmering texture behind the text
•	Overlaid botanical outlines fade in gently
•	Motion cue: “origin unfolding”

⸻

Section 3 — The Land & Sanctuary
•	City-grid pattern dissolves into organic leaf silhouettes
•	Water ripple layer appears briefly
•	Light gradient shifts mimic sunlight through trees
•	Motion cue: “transition from rush to calm”

⸻

Section 4 — Practices & Offerings

Each offering is not a box, but a ritual scene:
•	Sound healing: concentric wave lines pulse softly
•	Meditation: slow vertical rising lines
•	Energy work: flowing curve motion
•	Earthing: textured reveals mimicking earth cracking open
•	Workshops: mosaic-style layering effects
•	Motion cue: “every practice is a sensory invocation”

⸻

Section 5 — A Day at Kācima (Journey Scene)

Each stage transitions with atmospheric dissolves:
•	Arrival: light beams filter in
•	Grounding: dust-like particles settle
•	Immersion: vignette expands
•	Integration: warm blur dissolves
•	Return: fade to quiet olive tone
•	Motion cue: “a ritual sequence”

⸻

Section 6 — Community & Gatherings
•	Collage-style layering
•	Slow parallax of overlapping shapes
•	Deco frames fade in around quotes or future collaborators
•	Subtle grain pulses
•	Motion cue: “a living circle of people”

⸻

Section 7 — Visit & Connect
•	Root-to-line animation forms a minimal frame
•	Form fields fade in like written ink
•	Soft focus shift on scroll end
•	Motion cue: “closing the ceremony”

⸻

8. Responsive Design Rules
•	Mobile experience must retain narrative flow
•	Animations simplified but still atmospheric
•	Text sizes scale gently
•	Vertical stacking preferred over collapsing sections

⸻

9. Accessibility
•	Reduced motion mode disables parallax, pulses, dissolves
•	Color contrast maintained across clay/olive palette
•	Typography readable at all sizes
•	No flashing elements

⸻

10. Edge Cases & Fail States
•	Loading state uses slow-moving texture
•	Failed form submission uses soft clay-colored message
•	No harsh red error states

⸻

11. File & Asset Guidelines
•	All ornamental linework as SVG
•	All textures optimized as WebP
•	Use vector-based Lottie for line animations
•	All animations under performance budget
